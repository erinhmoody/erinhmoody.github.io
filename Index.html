Moby Dick Seafood Restaurant

A little known fact, Moby Dick has been a local, family owned Louisville institution since inception in 1967. At Moby Dick Third and Winkler we strive to maintain our original character, aka kitsch. You’ll also find family owners, employees since day one, neighborhood customers and those on their way to Churchill Downs. Your first mate sandwich, whale of a sandwich or box for two will come with hand cut, hand breaded cod. Those notorious onion rings, also cut and breaded by hand. Watch out for locally sourced fried green tomatoes when in season.

    When we moved from Louisville to New York City in 1990, I didn’t figure I’d miss our food. First day home we went straight to Moby Dick, an under-appreciated local mini-chain that, in my view, fries a fillet as well as anyone, and better than most. Sweet, flaky cod, cloaked in golden, crunchy cracker-crumb crust. Add simple sliced rye and a dab of tartar sauce, and our hearts warmed with Louisville comfort fare.
